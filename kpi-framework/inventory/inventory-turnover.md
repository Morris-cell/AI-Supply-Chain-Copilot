# Inventory Turnover

## Definition

Measures how many times inventory is sold and replenished during a period.

## Business Purpose

Evaluate inventory efficiency and working capital utilization.

## Formula

Inventory Turnover = COGS / Average Inventory
Average Inventory =(Beginning Inventory + Ending Inventory) / 2

## Required Business Concepts

- Cost of Goods Sold (Only recognized upon a sale)
- Inventory Value

## Interpretation

Higher:
- Faster inventory movement
- Lower carrying cost
- Strong sales or efficient purchasing
- Could be the result of insufficient inventory

Lower:
- Overstocking
- Weak demand
- Production decline

## Root Causes

1. Forecast Error
2. Excess Purchasing
3. Demand Decline
4. Product Obsolescence
5. Long Supplier Lead Time

## Improvement Actions

1. Improve Forecast Accuracy
2. Optimize Safety Stock
3. Reduce MOQ
4. Liquidate Obsolete Inventory

## Related KPIs

- Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO)
- Fill Rate
- Stockout Rate
- Forecast Accuracy
- Inventory Aging

## Data Requirements

Required Concepts:

- Cost of Goods Sold
- Beginning Inventory Value
- Ending Inventory Value

Optional Dimensions:

- Product
- Warehouse
- Business Unit
- Month
