# Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO)

## Definition

Measures the average number of days inventory remains in stock before being sold or consumed.

## Business Purpose

Evaluate inventory holding efficiency and the amount of working capital tied up in inventory.

## Formula

DIO = (Average Inventory / COGS) × Number of Days

Average Inventory = (Beginning Inventory + Ending Inventory) / 2

Commonly:

DIO = (Average Inventory / Annual COGS) × 365

## Required Business Concepts

- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
- Inventory Value
- Average Inventory
- Inventory Carrying Period

## Interpretation

Higher:
- Inventory stays longer before being sold
- More working capital tied up in inventory
- Higher holding and storage costs
- Potential risk of obsolescence or slow-moving stock

Lower:
- Faster inventory movement
- Better inventory utilization
- Lower carrying costs
- Could indicate risk of stockouts if inventory is too low

## Root Causes

1. Excess Inventory Purchasing
2. Demand Decline
3. Forecast Inaccuracy
4. Slow-Moving or Obsolete Products
5. Production Planning Issues
6. Long Customer Order Cycles
7. Seasonal Inventory Build-Up

## Improvement Actions

1. Improve Demand Forecast Accuracy
2. Optimize Reorder Point and Safety Stock
3. Reduce Excess Inventory Purchases
4. Increase Inventory Visibility
5. Accelerate Slow-Moving Inventory Clearance
6. Improve Sales and Promotion Planning
7. Implement Inventory Segmentation (ABC Analysis)
