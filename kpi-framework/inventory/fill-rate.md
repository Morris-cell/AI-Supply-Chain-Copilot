# Fill Rate

## Definition

Measures the percentage of customer demand that is fulfilled immediately from available inventory without backorders or lost sales.

## Business Purpose

Evaluate inventory availability and the organization's ability to satisfy customer demand at the time an order is placed.

## Formula

Fill Rate = (Delivered Quantity / Ordered Quantity) × 100%

## Required Business Concepts

### Mandatory

- Ordered Quantity
- Delivered Quantity

### Optional Dimensions

- Customer
- Product
- Warehouse
- Region
- Order Date
- Business Unit

## Interpretation

Higher Fill Rate indicates:

- High inventory availability
- Strong customer service performance
- Low stockout risk

Lower Fill Rate indicates:

- Inventory shortages
- Backorders
- Lost sales
- Poor replenishment planning

## Root Causes

1. Inventory shortage
2. Inaccurate demand forecasting
3. Long supplier lead time
4. Supplier delivery delays
5. Poor replenishment policy
6. Production shortages

## Improvement Actions

1. Improve demand forecasting
2. Optimize safety stock
3. Reduce supplier lead time
4. Improve supplier delivery performance
5. Optimize replenishment planning
6. Improve inventory visibility

## Related KPIs

- Inventory Turnover
- DIO

- OTIF
- OTD
- Supplier Lead Time
- Supplier On-Time Delivery

## Analysis Dependencies

### Previous Analysis

- Inventory Availability
- Supplier Lead Time
- Supplier On-Time Delivery

### Current Analysis

Evaluate whether customer demand can be fulfilled immediately from available inventory.

### Next Analysis

If Fill Rate decreases:

- Analyze Stockout Rate
- Review Inventory Aging
- Review Supplier Performance
- Review Forecast Accuracy
- Review Replenishment Policy

### Trigger Conditions

Proceed when:

- Fill Rate falls below target
- Customer backorders increase
- Lost sales increase
- Inventory shortages become frequent
