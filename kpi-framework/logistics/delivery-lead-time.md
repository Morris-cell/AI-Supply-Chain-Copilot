# Delivery Lead Time

## Definition

Measures the elapsed time between customer order creation and successful delivery to the customer.

## Business Purpose

Evaluate the efficiency and responsiveness of the end-to-end order fulfillment process.

## Formula

Delivery Lead Time = Actual Delivery Date − Order Date

The result is typically measured in days or hours.

## Required Business Concepts

### Mandatory

- Order Date
- Actual Delivery Date

### Optional Dimensions

- Customer
- Product
- Warehouse
- Carrier
- Transportation Mode
- Region
- Order Priority

## Interpretation

Lower Delivery Lead Time indicates:

- Faster order fulfillment
- Better customer responsiveness
- More efficient logistics operations

Higher Delivery Lead Time indicates:

- Delays in fulfillment
- Inefficient warehouse or transportation processes
- Reduced customer satisfaction

## Root Causes

1. Production delays
2. Warehouse processing delays
3. Transportation delays
4. Supplier delays
5. Customs or regulatory delays (international shipments)

## Improvement Actions

1. Improve warehouse picking and packing efficiency
2. Optimize transportation routes
3. Reduce order processing time
4. Improve supplier collaboration
5. Increase inventory availability

## Related KPIs

- OTD
- OTIF
- Transportation Cost
- Order Fulfillment Cycle Time
- Supplier Lead Time

## Analysis Dependencies

### Previous Analysis

- OTD

### Current Analysis

Measure the total time required to fulfill customer orders.

### Next Analysis

If Delivery Lead Time increases:

- Analyze Warehouse Processing Time
- Analyze Transportation Time
- Analyze Supplier Lead Time
- Review Order Processing Workflow

### Trigger Conditions

Proceed when:

- Delivery Lead Time exceeds target
- Delivery delays become more frequent
- Customer complaints regarding delivery speed increase
