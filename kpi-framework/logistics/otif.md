# OTIF (On Time In Full)

## Definition

Measures the percentage of customer orders delivered on time and in the correct quantity.

## Business Purpose

Evaluate delivery performance from the customer's perspective by combining delivery timeliness and delivery completeness into a single KPI.

## Formula

OTIF = (Number of Orders Delivered On Time and In Full / Total Number of Orders) × 100%

## Required Business Concepts

### Mandatory

* Requested Delivery Date
* Actual Delivery Date
* Ordered Quantity
* Delivered Quantity

### Optional Dimensions

* Customer
* Product
* Warehouse
* Carrier
* Region
* Order Date

## Interpretation

Higher OTIF indicates:

* Excellent customer service
* Reliable logistics execution
* Stable supply chain performance

Lower OTIF indicates:

* Delivery delays
* Partial shipments
* Inventory shortages
* Transportation issues

## Root Causes

1. Inventory shortage
2. Production delay
3. Transportation delay
4. Warehouse picking errors
5. Incorrect order allocation

## Improvement Actions

1. Improve inventory planning
2. Optimize warehouse operations
3. Improve transportation scheduling
4. Increase forecast accuracy
5. Strengthen supplier collaboration

## Related KPIs

* On-Time Delivery (OTD)
* Fill Rate
* Perfect Order Rate
* Inventory Turnover
* Forecast Accuracy

## Analysis Dependencies

### Previous Analysis

* Inventory Availability
* Fill Rate

### Current Analysis

Evaluate whether customer orders are delivered on time and in full.

### Next Analysis

If OTIF decreases:

* Analyze On-Time Delivery (OTD)
* Analyze Fill Rate
* Review Inventory Availability
* Review Carrier Performance
* Review Warehouse Performance

### Trigger Conditions

Proceed when:

* OTIF falls below target.
* Customer complaints increase.
* Late or partial deliveries become more frequent.
