# On-Time Delivery (OTD)

## Definition

Measures the percentage of customer orders delivered on or before the requested delivery date.

## Business Purpose

Evaluate delivery timeliness and logistics execution performance from the customer's perspective.

## Formula

OTD = (Number of Orders Delivered On Time / Total Number of Orders) × 100%

## Required Business Concepts

### Mandatory

- Requested Delivery Date
- Actual Delivery Date

### Optional Dimensions

- Customer
- Product
- Warehouse
- Carrier
- Region
- Order Date

## Interpretation

Higher OTD indicates:

- Reliable transportation performance
- Effective logistics planning
- Better customer satisfaction

Lower OTD indicates:

- Delivery delays
- Transportation issues
- Warehouse processing delays
- Production scheduling problems

## Root Causes

1. Transportation delays
2. Warehouse picking delays
3. Production delays
4. Carrier performance issues
5. Traffic or weather disruptions

## Improvement Actions

1. Optimize transportation routes
2. Improve warehouse efficiency
3. Monitor carrier performance
4. Improve production scheduling
5. Enhance shipment visibility

## Related KPIs

- OTIF
- Fill Rate
- Delivery Lead Time
- Carrier On-Time Performance

## Analysis Dependencies

### Previous Analysis

- OTIF

### Current Analysis

Determine whether delivery delays are contributing to poor customer service.

### Next Analysis

If OTD decreases:

- Analyze Carrier Performance
- Analyze Delivery Lead Time
- Review Warehouse Processing Time
- Review Transportation Routes

### Trigger Conditions

Proceed when:

- OTD falls below target
- Delivery delays increase
- Customer complaints about late deliveries increase
