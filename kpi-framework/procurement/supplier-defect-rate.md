# Supplier Defect Rate

## Definition

Measures the percentage of materials or purchase orders received from suppliers that fail quality inspection due to defects.

## Business Purpose

Evaluate supplier quality performance and identify suppliers that introduce quality risks into the supply chain.

## Formula

Supplier Defect Rate =
(Number of Defective Units Received / Total Units Received) × 100%

Alternative (PO-based)

Supplier Defect Rate =
(Number of Defective Purchase Orders / Total Purchase Orders) × 100%

## Required Business Concepts

### Mandatory

- Defective Quantity

- Business Unit

## Interpretation

Lower Supplier Defect Rate indicates:

- Better supplier quality
- More stable production
- Lower inspection and rework costs
- Higher customer satisfaction

Higher Supplier Defect Rate indicates:

- Poor supplier quality
- Increased production interruptions
- Higher scrap and rework costs
- Increased customer quality risk

## Root Causes

1. Supplier manufacturing defects
2. Poor raw material quality
3. Inadequate supplier quality control
4. Packaging damage
5. Transportation damage
6. Incorrect material specifications

## Improvement Actions

1. Strengthen supplier quality audits
2. Improve incoming quality inspection
3. Establish supplier quality scorecards
4. Require corrective action reports (CAPA)
5. Increase supplier collaboration
6. Develop alternative suppliers
