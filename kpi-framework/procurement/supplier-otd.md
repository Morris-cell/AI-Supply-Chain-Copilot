# Supplier On-Time Delivery (Supplier OTD)

## Definition

Measures the percentage of purchase orders received from suppliers on or before the promised delivery date.

## Business Purpose

Evaluate supplier delivery reliability and ensure stable material availability for production and inventory replenishment.

## Formula

Supplier OTD = (Number of Purchase Orders Received On Time / Total Purchase Orders) × 100%

## Required Business Concepts

### Mandatory

- Promised Delivery Date
- Goods Receipt Date

### Optional Dimensions

- Supplier
- Material
- Purchase Order
- Plant
- Buyer
- Country
- Business Unit

## Interpretation

Higher Supplier OTD indicates:

- Reliable supplier performance
- Stable procurement operations
- Lower risk of material shortages

Lower Supplier OTD indicates:

- Frequent supplier delivery delays
- Increased production risk
- Higher safety stock requirements

## Root Causes

1. Supplier production delays
2. Transportation disruptions
3. Customs clearance delays
4. Capacity shortages
5. Poor supplier scheduling

## Improvement Actions

1. Monitor supplier performance regularly
2. Develop supplier scorecards
3. Diversify supplier base
4. Improve supplier collaboration
5. Strengthen delivery commitment management

## Related KPIs

- Supplier Lead Time
- OTIF

## Analysis Dependencies

### Previous Analysis

- Supplier Lead Time

### Current Analysis

Evaluate supplier delivery reliability against committed delivery dates.

### Next Analysis

If Supplier OTD decreases:

- Review delayed suppliers
- Analyze transportation performance
- Analyze material shortages
- Review supplier capacity
- Assess inventory impact

### Trigger Conditions

Proceed when:

- Supplier OTD falls below target
- Late supplier deliveries increase
- Production interruptions occur
