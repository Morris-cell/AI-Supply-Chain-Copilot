# Supplier Lead Time

## Definition

Measures the average time taken by a supplier to fulfill a purchase order, from order placement to goods receipt.

## Business Purpose

Evaluate supplier responsiveness and procurement efficiency to ensure stable material availability.

## Formula

Supplier Lead Time = Goods Receipt Date − Purchase Order Date

Average Supplier Lead Time =
Total Supplier Lead Time / Number of Purchase Orders

## Required Business Concepts

### Mandatory

- Purchase Order Date
- Goods Receipt Date

### Optional Dimensions

- Supplier
- Material
- Purchase Order
- Plant
- Buyer
- Country
- Business Unit

## Interpretation

Shorter Supplier Lead Time indicates:

- Responsive suppliers
- Better material availability
- Lower safety stock requirements

Longer Supplier Lead Time indicates:

- Supplier delays
- Procurement risk
- Higher inventory requirements
- Potential production disruption

## Root Causes

1. Supplier production delays
2. Transportation delays
3. Customs clearance delays
4. Raw material shortages
5. Poor supplier planning

## Improvement Actions

1. Improve supplier collaboration
2. Monitor supplier performance
3. Diversify supplier base
4. Improve purchase planning
5. Reduce transportation bottlenecks
