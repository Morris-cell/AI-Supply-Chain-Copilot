# Forecast Bias

## Definition

Measures whether demand forecasts consistently overestimate or underestimate actual demand.

## Business Purpose

Evaluate systematic forecasting errors and identify whether demand planning tends to over-forecast or under-forecast.

## Formula

Forecast Bias (%) =
((Forecast Quantity − Actual Demand Quantity) / Actual Demand Quantity) × 100%

Interpretation:

- Positive Value → Over Forecast
- Negative Value → Under Forecast
- Zero → Unbiased Forecast

## Required Business Concepts

### Mandatory

- Forecast Quantity
- Actual Demand Quantity

### Optional Dimensions

- Product
- Product Category
- Customer
- Region
- Forecast Version
- Forecast Period
- Business Unit

## Interpretation

Positive Forecast Bias indicates:

- Forecasts are consistently higher than actual demand
- Risk of excess inventory
- Higher carrying costs
- Increased inventory aging

Negative Forecast Bias indicates:

- Forecasts are consistently lower than actual demand
- Higher stockout risk
- Lost sales
- Lower customer service level

Forecast Bias close to zero indicates:

- Balanced forecasting process
- No systematic over- or under-forecasting

## Root Causes

1. Overly optimistic sales expectations
2. Conservative forecasting practices
3. Promotion assumptions
4. Poor demand planning process
5. Historical demand anomalies
6. Inadequate collaboration between departments

## Improvement Actions

1. Review forecasting assumptions
2. Improve Sales & Operations Planning (S&OP)
3. Monitor forecast bias monthly
4. Improve collaboration between Sales and Supply Chain
5. Segment products by demand pattern

### Trigger Conditions

Proceed when:

- Forecast Bias exceeds acceptable tolerance
- Excess inventory increases
